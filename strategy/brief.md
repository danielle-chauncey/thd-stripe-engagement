# The Brief (verbatim)

## The Scenario

The Home Depot is the world's largest home improvement retailer, with over $150 billion in annual revenue, 2,300+ stores across North America, and rapidly growing digital and contractor businesses. They serve two core customer segments — the DIY consumer and the professional contractor ("Pro") — and are in the midst of a significant technology and payments modernization effort to unify their in-store, online, and B2B commerce experiences.

Our goal is to demonstrate how Stripe can partner with The Home Depot through their digital transformation journey. We need to ensure their Executive team views Stripe as not only the right solution partner today, but also the relevant innovation partner for the long term — based on our market-leading roadmap and ecosystem of solutions.

## Your Task

Create an executive engagement plan for strategic touch points throughout 2026 that can elevate The Home Depot's view of payments as a business-critical initiative — something that is seen as transformational, not transactional — and that builds lasting relationships between their team and Stripe.

Your plan should include milestones, timelines, and ways content can be customized for each audience and situation.

## Things to Consider

### Pre-Engagement
- How do you go about collaborating with the internal Stripe team members who currently manage The Home Depot relationship? And how do you identify and mobilize new Stripe stakeholders in the future to evolve the relationship?
- How do you gather the right information from The Home Depot to ensure you're aligned on the content strategy for each executive touch point?
- What would you send to The Home Depot prior to a major engagement (e.g., an Executive Briefing Center visit) to get them up to speed on Stripe and set expectations?
- What is the best way to prepare them for individual virtual experiences vs. in-person moments?
- What could you do to get The Home Depot excited about engaging with Stripe — is there anything worth sending in advance to build anticipation or frame the value proposition?

### Engagement: Build an Agenda for a Key Executive Touch Point
(Example: An Executive Briefing at Stripe HQ)
- Who from The Home Depot would you want in the room — and why those specific roles?
- Who from Stripe would you want to participate? Would you identify or pursue an Executive Sponsor strategy for the THD account?
- What content themes would resonate most with The Home Depot's executive priorities? What are the key messages about Stripe you'd want them to walk away with?
- Where do you see natural cultural or business alignment between Stripe and The Home Depot that could be leveraged to build rapport?
- Which Stripe executives would you want to involve, and what role would each play in the engagement?
- How do you keep the content dynamic and the audience engaged throughout the session?
- What's your recommendation on format — in-person, virtual, or hybrid — and what's the rationale?
- Are there any "wow factor" moments, unique experiences, or creative elements you'd incorporate?

### Post-Engagement
- What feedback would you want to capture after the engagement, and how would you gather it?
- How do you mutually define and agree on next steps with The Home Depot team?
- What does the post-engagement journey look like for the Stripe sales team to capitalize on the momentum and accelerate the deal cycle?

If there are additional elements you'd like to include at any stage of the engagement plan, please do — we want to see how you think beyond the brief.
