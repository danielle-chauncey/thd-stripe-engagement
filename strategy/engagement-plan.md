# Stripe → The Home Depot
## 2026 Executive Engagement Plan — A Content Strategy

> **The bet:** The Home Depot's executives should walk away seeing payments not as a line item they negotiate down, but as the connective tissue of the interconnected retailer they're already building. Transformational, not transactional. Everything below is engineered to move that view.

*Author: Danielle Chauncey. Built on public research (see `research/`) and patterns drawn from a decade running large-scale executive engagement programs — customer advisory boards, executive summits, and executive briefing programs. Fully fictional case exercise; all facts from public sources; no confidential data.*

---

## 0. How to read this plan

This is a **content strategy**, not a program calendar. Real Executive Briefings often come together in 48 hours; what has to be excellent in that window is the *content* — what's in the room, customized to who's sitting there and why.

So the plan is built as a **content system**:
- **One thesis**, stated once (Section 1).
- **A modular content kit** — pre-built blocks that snap together fast (Section 2).
- **Customization, cut primarily by role**, then by format and moment (Section 3).
- Assembled into a **flagship EBC at Stripe HQ** (Section 5), and re-assemblable for any virtual or 48-hour moment.
- Wrapped in a **lightweight 2026 arc** (Section 7) — the spine that serves the content, not the other way around.

The three phases the brief asks for — **Pre-Engagement (4), Engagement (5), Post-Engagement (6)** — each answer every sub-question explicitly.

## 1. The thesis (the one idea everything customizes off)

**The Home Depot is becoming one interconnected retailer. Its payments should become one interconnected system — and that system is a growth engine, not a cost center.**

THD has said this in its own words. The "One Home Depot" interconnected-retail strategy blends physical and digital for DIY and Pro. CEO Ted Decker calls THD a "project retailer" investing to "remove friction and make home improvement seamless." New CTO Fran Bell is mandated to drive "a seamless, interconnected, data-driven experience across associates, DIYers, and Pros."

Payments is where that vision either becomes real or stays fragmented. Today most large retailers run in-store, online, and B2B on different payment stacks that don't talk to each other. That fragmentation is invisible on a P&L and enormous in impact: lost sales from failed authorizations, fraud handled three different ways, no unified view of a customer who shops in all three channels, and a Pro contractor whose financial relationship resets every time he changes channel.

**Stripe's role:** unify it. One platform across in-store (Terminal), online (Payments), and B2B (Billing/Invoicing), with embedded finance (Issuing, Capital, Treasury) purpose-built for the Pro. Turn payments data into the connective tissue of the interconnected retailer. That is the transformation. Everything in this plan is a different way of telling that one story to a different person at a different moment.

## 2. The modular content kit

Five reusable blocks. Each is built once, kept current, and personalized fast. A 48-hour EBC is "pick the blocks, swap in this audience's priorities, rehearse." A year-long arc is "sequence the blocks across moments." This modularity is the answer to *both* "how do you customize content per audience" and "how do you move fast."

| # | Block | What it is | The transformational move |
|---|---|---|---|
| **B1** | **The THD Data Story** | THD's own public numbers turned into a payments-opportunity narrative: $164.7B sales, 2,359 stores + 1,250 SRS, Pro ecosystem, interconnected-retail strategy → where fragmented payments silently cost them and where unification creates growth. | Reframes payments from "what we pay processors" to "where our next points of margin and growth are hiding." |
| **B2** | **The Demo on Their Own Flows** | Not a generic Stripe demo. A live walk-through of Stripe running a *THD-shaped* flow: a Pro contractor checkout that follows him across app → store → invoice; a self-checkout authorization; a cross-channel return. | Makes "interconnected" tangible. They see their own customer, not a Stripe logo. |
| **B3** | **Peer Proof** | The URBN story: consolidated **$5B of online + in-store revenue onto one Stripe platform** across 600–700 stores — universal payment token, cross-channel refunds, BOPIS/BOSS. Plus quantified outcomes. | "A retailer your size already did the thing we're describing. Here's what it got them." De-risks the bet. |
| **B4** | **The Roadmap POV** | Stripe's forward view: where payments + AI + embedded finance are going, and why Stripe is the long-term *innovation* partner — agentic commerce, optimization that compounds, the platform that ships new capability without re-integration. | Answers "relevant innovation partner for the long term," not just "right solution today." Speaks directly to Bell's AI mandate. |
| **B5** | **The Customized Pre-Read / Anticipation Piece** | What's sent in advance (Section 4). The data story + agenda + "what Stripe sees in your business," personalized per recipient. | Frames the value before they walk in; gets them invested and excited. |

**Content principles** (ported from the executive briefing discipline, repurposed as content rules — these are what make content land fast and feel bespoke):
1. **Assume zero prior context.** Every claim self-contained; every reference explained.
2. **Quantify every proof point.** Never "we help retailers." Always the named retailer and the number.
3. **Lead with their priorities, not our products.** Open on THD's stated strategy; arrive at Stripe as the means.
4. **Script the objective.** Each piece has one outcome, the talking points to reach it, and one specific ask.
5. **Decidedly un-salesy.** Frame as "shape what we build / here's what we see," not a pitch. This is the single biggest differentiator from a vendor QBR.

| Moment | Content job | Dominant block | The ask |
|---|---|---|---|

**How a single piece of content gets built:** pick the block(s) → apply the role cut → apply the format shape → apply the moment's ask. Four decisions, minutes not days, because the blocks already exist. *This is the system the brief is really asking for.*

## 4. PRE-ENGAGEMENT

*Answers every Pre-Engagement sub-question in the brief.*

### 4.1 Collaborating with the internal Stripe team that owns THD today
- **Start with a relationship-mapping session.** Pull the current account team (AE, SE, account exec sponsor) and build a shared map: who at Stripe touches THD, who at THD they touch, relationship temperature, open threads, last 12 months of history. The engagement plan is co-owned, never parachuted in over the existing team.
- **Establish a single source of truth** for the account narrative so every Stripe person tells the same story (the thesis in Section 1). Misaligned narrative is the fastest way to look transactional.
- **Agree on roles before content.** Who owns the relationship, who owns content, who owns the exec sponsor motion (Section 5.2).

### 4.2 Identifying and mobilizing NEW Stripe stakeholders over time
- **Map Stripe stakeholders to THD's org as it evolves.** THD just created a CTO role (Bell) — Stripe should have a counterpart-level technologist ready to engage her. As THD's Pro and online orgs grow, line up Stripe product and enterprise leaders against Rowe and Broggi.
- **Mobilize by moment, not by title.** Bring a Stripe exec in when there's a specific reason (a roadmap reveal, a peer intro, a decision to unblock), brief them tightly, and give them a defined role (Section 5.5). Executives engage when the ask is specific and the prep is done for them.

### 4.3 Gathering the right information from THD to align content strategy
The four public-research gaps become the **discovery agenda** (we don't invent answers; we go ask):
1. **THD's actual payments stack** — in-store POS, online gateway, B2B/trade-credit systems, incumbent processors. (Not public.)
2. **Pro's exact revenue share + growth, and SRS/HD Supply integration** — sizes the Pro prize.
3. **THD's stated B2B trade-credit / Pro Xtra financing initiatives** — where embedded finance maps.
4. **THD's definition of "friction"** — what Decker and Bell actually mean operationally; what "seamless" looks like to them.

**How to gather it:** a structured discovery conversation with the account team's THD contacts, framed as "help us build content around what matters to you," plus a short pre-engagement input request to attendees (Section 4.5). Every content block is then tuned to real answers.

### 4.4 What to send before a major engagement (e.g., the EBC) to set expectations
A **pre-read package** (B5), sent ~2 weeks out:
- **The agenda-at-a-glance** (detailed run-of-show held for arrival — builds intimacy).
- **A Stripe primer** that assumes zero context: who Stripe is, the scale stats, the URBN proof — calibrated so a first-time exec is up to speed.
- **The "what Stripe sees in your business" piece** (the anticipation hook, 4.5).
- **Logistics framed separately** in a "Know Before You Go" (~1 week out): location, security, format, attire, dinner — so logistics never crowd out content.
- **Explicit expectation-setting:** "This is a working session built around your priorities, not a presentation. Come ready to react and direct."

### 4.5 Preparing them for virtual vs. in-person (different motions)
- **In-person:** a virtual prep touch ~2–4 weeks out to set the agenda and what "good participation" looks like; the pre-read; hold the detailed agenda for arrival.
- **Virtual:** ruthless brevity and engineered interactivity — mandatory tech check, everyone on camera, a dedicated Stripe chat-monitor feeding questions, 2–3 pre-seeded questions, open with the confidentiality frame and explicit permission to interrupt. Always record and send to absentees as a hook into the next moment.
- **The rule:** in-person earns high-touch staging; virtual earns brevity + interactivity + recording-led follow-up. Never run a virtual moment as a shrunk in-person one.

### 4.6 Building anticipation / framing the value prop in advance
- **"What Stripe sees in your business" (the signature anticipation piece).** A short, personalized artifact built from THD's own public numbers and stated strategy: here's where we see payments quietly costing you, here's the growth hiding in unification, here's a retailer your size who already did it. It frames the value *before* they walk in and makes the EBC feel built for them. This is the "beyond docs/pptx" object — rendered as something they actually want to open (see the interactive site).
- **A personal invitation from a senior Stripe executive**, framing the session as a hand-built working forum, not a sales meeting.
- **A participation hook:** ask each attendee, in advance, for their single biggest payments priority or question — so they co-author the agenda and arrive invested.
- **Tease one marquee moment** (a Collison fireside, an exclusive roadmap preview, the demo on their own flows) by name.

## 5. ENGAGEMENT — The flagship EBC at Stripe HQ

*Answers every Engagement sub-question. This is the centerpiece the content kit assembles for; the same blocks re-assemble for the 48-hour or virtual version.*

### 5.1 Who from THD in the room — and why
Anchor on the **technology-and-transformation core**, balanced with the **business owners** so the room makes decisions, not just listens:

| Attendee | Why they're essential |
|---|---|
| **Fran Bell, CTO** *(keystone)* | Her mandate *is* the thesis. The session is, in large part, built to arm her agenda. |
| **Angie Brown, CIO** | Owns the stack decision and integration reality. Unification is hers to execute. |
| **Richard McPhail, CFO** | Turns "transformational" into a funded business case. Without finance, vision stalls. |
| **Michael Rowe, EVP Pro** | The Pro ecosystem is the highest-growth, most differentiated payments opportunity. |
| **Jordan Broggi, EVP CX / President Online** | Owns conversion and the cross-channel journey — where unification shows up for customers. |
| **Ted Decker, CEO** *(for a marquee segment)* | A focused CEO-to-founder segment sets the relationship altitude. Not the whole day. |

Rationale: payments unification is a **cross-functional decision** (tech + finance + the two growth P&Ls). Putting them in one room, hearing one thesis, is itself the transformational act — it reframes payments as an enterprise strategy, not an IT procurement.

### 5.2 Stripe participants + Executive Sponsor strategy
**Yes — pursue a formal Executive Sponsor strategy.** A named Stripe executive owns the THD relationship at the top, shows up at the milestone moments, and is the personal escalation path. This is what signals "strategic partner" and sustains the relationship between touch points. (Stripe's specific exec-engagement leaders aren't public — naming the sponsor is an internal step; the *strategy* is to assign one and make the pairing CTO-to-senior-technologist and CEO-to-founder.)

### 5.3 Content themes that resonate + key walk-away messages
**Themes** (mapped to their stated priorities): interconnected retail made real through unified payments; the Pro as a payments-and-finance growth engine; payments data as fuel for AI; reliability and optimization at peak; innovation partnership for the long term.

**Three walk-away messages:**
1. **"Payments is strategic infrastructure for One Home Depot"** — the connective tissue, not a cost line.
2. **"Stripe is the right partner today"** — proven at our scale (URBN $5B, 99.999% uptime), ready now.
3. **"Stripe is the innovation partner for the long term"** — the programmable, AI-forward platform that compounds (Bell's mandate).

### 5.4 Cultural / business alignment for rapport
- **Entrepreneurial Spirit** — an explicit THD core value; it's also Stripe's founding ethos. Genuine shared DNA: builders who innovate to serve the customer.
- **"Show, don't just sell"** — THD's founding service philosophy. Mirror it: don't pitch, *show* (the demo on their own flows). Let the rapport be "we work like you do."
- **Removing friction / "whatever it takes"** — Decker's language and THD's heritage. Stripe's entire value prop is removing friction. Speak their words back to them.
- **Project retailer** — adopt their framing; talk in projects and customers, not transactions and rails.

### 5.5 Which Stripe executives, and each one's role
| Stripe exec | Role in the engagement |
|---|---|
| **Executive Sponsor (senior enterprise leader)** | Owns the room, opens with the thesis, holds the relationship after. |
| **A Collison (founder) — marquee segment** | The vision + innovation conversation with Decker/Bell. The "wow," the altitude-setter. |
| **Senior product/technology leader** | Bell's counterpart; the roadmap POV (B4) and the AI-forward future. |
| **Solutions/enterprise architect** | Drives the demo on their own flows (B2); makes it real and technical. |
| **Account team (AE/SE)** | Continuity, follow-through, the people THD already trusts; co-own every moment. |

### 5.6 Keeping content dynamic and the audience engaged
- **Demo-led, not slide-led.** The center of gravity is B2 — Stripe running their own flows live.
- **Discussion over presentation** — 60%+ dialogue; open each segment by reflecting back the priorities they submitted in advance.
- **Role-routed segments** — each segment clearly speaks to someone in the room, so no one is a spectator.
- **A facilitator distinct from presenters**; pre-seeded questions; named hand-offs.
- **The thesis as a through-line** — every segment ties back to "one interconnected system."

### 5.7 Format recommendation + rationale
**In-person, hybrid-capable.** In-person for the flagship: the demo, the founder moment, and a cross-functional THD group in one room can't be replicated virtually — presence *is* the message that this is strategic. Build hybrid capability so a key THD exec who can't travel still joins the marquee segments live, and so every segment is recorded for the pre-read/follow-up library. **Virtual** is reserved for the deep-dives between in-person moments (Section 3b).

### 5.8 "Wow factor" moments
- **The demo on their own flows (B2)** — seeing a THD Pro followed seamlessly across app → store → invoice is the visceral "oh, *that's* what interconnected payments means" moment.
- **"What Stripe sees in your business" reveal** — open the session with their own numbers reframed as opportunity; it signals the whole thing was built for them.
- **Founder fireside** — a Collison with Decker/Bell on the future of commerce and AI. Scarce, memorable, altitude-setting.
- **A forward artifact they keep** — a tangible "vision of One Home Depot payments in 2028" they can carry back internally (extends Stripe's reach beyond the room).

## 6. POST-ENGAGEMENT

*Answers every Post-Engagement sub-question.*

### 6.1 Feedback to capture, and how
- **Capture:** Did the thesis land? Which block resonated per role? What objections or new priorities surfaced? Who's the internal champion? What's the appetite for a pilot?
- **How:** a short structured debrief with the Stripe team within 24 hours (while memory is fresh), a light feedback ask to THD attendees (2–3 questions, not a survey grind), and — most valuable — **1:1 follow-up calls** with key attendees (especially Bell and the champion) to gather candid reactions the room won't surface.

### 6.2 Mutually defining next steps with THD
- **Don't end the session without a named, dated next step** — the cardinal rule. Co-define it in the room: a scoped pilot, a technical deep-dive, a discovery on their actual stack (the 4.3 gaps).
- **Make it mutual** — THD names an owner, Stripe names an owner, both commit to a date. A next step only one side owns isn't a next step.
- **Tie it to their decision timeline** (budget cycles, peak season, board moments) so it has real urgency.

### 6.3 The post-engagement journey for the Stripe sales team
- **Within 48 hours:** thank-you + recap + the forward artifact + the agreed next step in writing.
- **Convert momentum to a scoped pilot** on a real THD flow (e.g., a Pro checkout or a single-channel optimization) — small enough to start fast, real enough to prove the thesis on their data.
- **Bracket with 1:1 exec connects** — the exec sponsor stays personally engaged; the CTO-counterpart keeps the technical thread warm.
- **Instrument against business signals** — authorization-rate lift, conversion, fraud reduction — so every later conversation is evidence-based and the deal cycle accelerates on proof, not promises.
- **Feed learnings back into the content kit** — what landed becomes the next moment's content; the system compounds.

## 7. The 2026 arc (the lightweight spine)

Not a program calendar — the minimum structure that turns moments into a relationship with momentum. Every touch teases and dates the next; the year builds to a capstone.

| Quarter | Moment | Format | Dominant blocks | Job |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Q1–Q2** | First touch + discovery | Virtual + account-team | B1, B5 | Frame the opportunity; run discovery; earn the EBC. |
| **Q2–Q3** | **The flagship EBC at Stripe HQ** | In-person (hybrid) | Full kit | The transformational moment; secure a pilot. |
| **Q3** | Pilot + technical deep-dives | Virtual deep-dives | B2, B4 | Prove it on their data; keep Bell/Brown engaged. |
| **Q4** | Capstone (expansion + sponsor) | In-person or marquee virtual | B3, B4 | Commit to the bet; name the exec sponsor; expand scope. |

**Momentum mechanics:** multi-touch participation goal (each key exec engaged in ≥2 moments), a mid-year recalibration of H2 content from what landed in H1, shareable takeaways after each moment, and the exec sponsor sustaining 1:1 contact between touch points.

## 8. Beyond the brief (where I'd push further)

- **The content kit as a durable asset.** The five blocks outlive any one meeting — they become THD's living engagement library, kept current, re-cut on demand. That's how a 2-day EBC stays excellent: the system is always warm.
- **"What Stripe sees in your business" as a repeatable motion.** The personalized, public-data-driven anticipation piece is reusable for any strategic account — THD is the showcase.
- **CTO-timed entry.** Bell starting in April 2026 with an interconnected-AI mandate is a once-a-decade timing gift. The plan deliberately orbits a brand-new executive building her agenda — Stripe should be a primary input to it.
- **Payments data as the AI story.** Bell's agentic-AI mandate needs clean, unified, real-time transaction data. Fragmented payments starve AI; unified payments feed it. That's the bridge from "payments" to "the future you're chartered to build" — and the deepest version of transformational, not transactional.
